✨ Cool & Unique Things To Do

Explorations worth your time — outdoorsy, walkable, budget-friendly with a few small costs sprinkled in.

🌊 LA & South Bay
🏔️
Portuguese Bend Reserve
Miles of coastal sage scrub trails in Rancho Palos Verdes with panoramic Pacific views. One of the most scenic hikes in all of LA that most people don't know about — peaceful, wild-feeling, and close by.
FreeOutdoorHike
🐋
Point Vicente Lighthouse Bluffs
Stand on RPV cliffs and watch for gray whales and dolphins near the Point Vicente Interpretive Center. July is a great month for dolphin sightings. Free, stunning, and you can combine it with the adjacent hiking trails.
FreeOutdoorWalk
🌊
Leo Carrillo Sea Caves & Tide Pools
Drive 28 miles up PCH from Santa Monica to reach a beach with actual sea caves you can walk into at low tide plus rich tide pools. More wild and interesting than a typical sandy beach. One of Malibu's best-kept secrets.
$8 parkingOutdoorWater
🌿
Franklin Canyon Park
A secret 605-acre wilderness park hidden in the hills between Beverly Hills and the Valley, with a gorgeous 3-acre duck lake that feels like it shouldn't exist in LA. Filming location for countless classic films. Free, uncrowded, and magical.
FreeOutdoorHidden gem
🪜
Silver Lake Secret Stairways
LA has a network of hidden outdoor public staircases — Silver Lake has some of the best, doubling as an open-air art gallery with murals. A perfect 90-minute wander through a neighborhood most tourists never find.
FreeWalkHidden gem
🌅
El Matador State Beach
The most dramatic beach in Malibu — sea stacks, rock arches, and secluded coves that look like Iceland but are 45 minutes from Hollywood. Go early for golden hour light on the rocks. One of LA's best-kept secrets.
$8 parkingOutdoorBeachHidden gem
🌸
South Coast Botanic Garden
87 acres of themed gardens in RPV — rose garden, cactus section, Mediterranean plants. Built on a former landfill and now one of the most beautiful green spaces in LA. Great for a slow, peaceful morning wander.
~$15 entryOutdoorWalk
🚣
Echo Park Lake Swan Boats
Rent a classic swan pedal boat on Echo Park Lake with views of the LA skyline and lotus flowers floating around you. Deeply old-school and charming — one of those genuinely fun LA experiences that feels nothing like the city around it.
Cheap rentalOutdoorWater
🔭
Griffith Observatory
Touristy but for good reason — free entry, incredible 360° views of LA, free planetarium shows (book ahead). Hike up from Los Feliz for extra credit. Best at dusk when the city lights up below you.
Free entryOutdoorHikeable
🎨
Abbot Kinney Blvd, Venice
"The coolest block in America" per GQ. A mile-long stretch of independent boutiques, galleries, street food, and murals. Walk it on a weekend afternoon — great people-watching, architecture, and spontaneous stops.
Free to walkWalkOutdoor
🌁 San Francisco
🎵
The Wave Organ
A wave-activated acoustic sculpture built from reclaimed marble and pipes that literally plays music using the motion of the ocean. Sit inside the chambers and listen. Near the Marina — completely free and totally surreal.
FreeOutdoorHidden gem
🌊
Lands End Trail + Rock Labyrinth
Coastal trail through cypress forests with sweeping Golden Gate views, ending at a hidden rock labyrinth on the shoreline (rebuilt by volunteers). One of the most magical free things you can do in SF. Wear layers.
FreeOutdoorTrailHidden gem
🌿
Sutro Heights Park
Hidden park at the edge of the city overlooking Cliff House and the Pacific. Ruins of the old Sutro estate remain — crumbling stone walls, overgrown statues, ocean views. Locals love it. Tourists almost never find it.
FreeOutdoorHidden gem
🚣
Stow Lake Rowboats — Golden Gate Park
Rent a rowboat or pedal boat on a peaceful island lake inside Golden Gate Park. Circle the island, watch the ducks and herons, feel completely removed from the city. Cheap, fun, and quietly perfect.
Cheap rentalOutdoorWater
⛰️
Twin Peaks at Dusk
Short walk or drive to the top of Twin Peaks for 360° panoramic views of the entire city and bay. Sunset or just after is spectacular — free, easy, and one of the best views in SF without hiking far. Castro neighborhood is right below.
FreeOutdoorShort hike
🕹️
Musée Mécanique
Free-to-enter museum at Fisherman's Wharf packed with hundreds of fully operational antique arcade games from the 1800s. Bring a few dollars in coins to play them. One of the most delightfully bizarre and fun places in SF.
Free entryUnique
🌈
Dolores Park
THE park in SF — right in the Mission/Castro area. On a sunny day it's one of the most vibrant, joyful outdoor spaces in the country. Bring snacks, lie in the grass, soak in the incredible city and bay views from the hill.
FreeOutdoorWalk
🌲
Ina Coolbrith Park
Hidden hilltop garden on Russian Hill with sweeping city views that almost no tourists visit. Named after California's first poet laureate. Terraced stairways through lush greenery — the kind of place that makes you feel like a SF local.
FreeOutdoorHidden gem
